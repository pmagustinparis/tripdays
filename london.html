How Many Days in London?

5-7 days recommended

Why Visit London?

London, one of the world's most visited cities, offers an incredible blend of history, culture, and modernity. This dynamic metropolis boasts iconic landmarks that span centuries, from the medieval Tower of London to the contemporary Shard skyscraper. With its world-class museums (many offering free admission), diverse neighborhoods, royal heritage, and vibrant arts scene, London provides endless opportunities for exploration. The city's multicultural population has created a culinary paradise with cuisine from around the world, while its historic pubs, royal parks, and famous shopping districts add to its charm. Whether you're watching the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace, exploring the British Museum's treasures, or enjoying a West End show, London delivers an unforgettable experience that justifies its reputation as a global capital.

How Many Days Do You Need in London?

To properly experience London, we recommend 5-7 days. This duration allows you to visit the major attractions, explore different neighborhoods, enjoy the cultural offerings, and perhaps take a day trip to nearby destinations like Windsor Castle or Oxford. If you only have 3-4 days, you can still see the highlights, but you'll need to be selective. With a week or more, you can discover London at a more relaxed pace and venture beyond the tourist hotspots.

London Itinerary: Day by Day

Day 1: Historic London

Begin your London adventure with its iconic historic sites. Start at Westminster Abbey, the coronation church of British monarchs, then walk to Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament. Cross Westminster Bridge for views of the Thames and visit the London Eye for panoramic city vistas. Continue to Trafalgar Square and the National Gallery. End your day with a walk through St. James's Park to Buckingham Palace.

Day 2: Royal London

Dedicate this day to London's royal heritage. Begin at the Tower of London to see the Crown Jewels and learn about its fascinating history. Cross Tower Bridge for spectacular views, then explore the south bank of the Thames. Visit HMS Belfast if you're interested in naval history. In the afternoon, head to Kensington Palace and stroll through the beautiful Kensington Gardens and Hyde Park.

Day 3: Museums and Culture

London's museums are world-class and many are free. Start at the British Museum to see treasures from around the world, including the Rosetta Stone and the Elgin Marbles. After lunch, visit South Kensington for the Victoria and Albert Museum (decorative arts), the Natural History Museum (dinosaurs and nature), and the Science Museum. End your day with a show in London's famous West End theater district.

Day 4: Markets and Modern London

Experience London's vibrant markets and contemporary areas. Begin at Borough Market for delicious food and local produce. Walk along the Thames to the Tate Modern art gallery in a converted power station. Cross the Millennium Bridge to St. Paul's Cathedral. In the afternoon, explore the trendy Shoreditch area with its street art, boutiques, and cafés. Visit Spitalfields Market and Brick Lane for vintage finds and global cuisine.

Day 5: Day Trip

Take a day trip from London to expand your British experience. Options include Windsor Castle (the oldest inhabited castle in the world), historic Oxford with its prestigious university, prehistoric Stonehenge, or the charming spa town of Bath with its Roman baths. All are accessible by train or organized tour and make for a fascinating contrast to London's urban landscape.

Day 6: North London

Discover North London's attractions. Start at the British Library to see historical documents including the Magna Carta. Visit Camden Market for alternative fashion, crafts, and international street food. Explore the beautiful Regent's Park and perhaps visit the London Zoo. Continue to Primrose Hill for panoramic views of the city skyline. End your day in Hampstead Heath, a vast park with swimming ponds and woodland walks.

Day 7: Hidden Gems

On your final day, discover some of London's lesser-known treasures. Visit Greenwich via river boat to stand on the Prime Meridian at the Royal Observatory, explore the National Maritime Museum, and see the Cutty Sark ship. Alternatively, explore the Notting Hill area with its colorful houses and Portobello Road Market. End your London adventure with a sunset view from Sky Garden or The Shard.

Top Attractions in London

  • British Museum - World-class collection of art and antiquities
  • Tower of London - Historic castle and home to the Crown Jewels
  • Buckingham Palace - Official residence of the British monarch
  • Westminster Abbey - Gothic church and coronation site
  • Big Ben & Houses of Parliament - Iconic symbols of London
  • London Eye - Giant observation wheel with panoramic views
  • Tate Modern - Contemporary art in a former power station
  • St. Paul's Cathedral - Sir Christopher Wren's masterpiece
  • Natural History Museum - Fascinating exhibits in a stunning building
  • Camden Market - Vibrant market with unique goods and food

Practical Tips for Visiting London

Best Time to Visit

The best times to visit London are March to May or September to November when the weather is mild and crowds are smaller. Summer (June-August) brings warmer temperatures but larger crowds and higher prices. Winter offers festive decorations and January sales, but shorter days and often rainy weather.

Getting Around

London has an extensive public transportation system. The Underground (Tube) is the fastest way to travel, while buses offer scenic routes. Purchase an Oyster card or use a contactless payment card for the best fares. Consider the Hopper fare which allows multiple bus journeys within an hour for a single fare. Walking is ideal for central areas, and Santander Cycles offer bike rentals throughout the city.

Budget Planning

Expect to spend about £120-180 per day per person for mid-range travel, including accommodation, meals, attractions, and transportation. Save money by visiting the many free museums, purchasing a London Pass for multiple paid attractions, and using meal deals from supermarkets. Budget accommodations start around £80 per night, while mid-range hotels range from £120-250.

Local Etiquette

Stand on the right side of escalators to allow people to pass on the left. Queue (line up) for services - Brits take queuing seriously! Tipping is customary in restaurants (10-15%) if service isn't included, but not necessary in pubs. A simple "please" and "thank you" goes a long way in interactions.

Is London Worth Visiting?

London offers an unparalleled mix of history, culture, and modernity that few cities can match. With its world-class (and often free) museums, iconic landmarks, diverse neighborhoods, and excellent transportation system, London provides a wealth of experiences for every type of traveler. While 5-7 days gives you a good introduction to the city, London's depth means you could spend weeks exploring and still discover new treasures.
