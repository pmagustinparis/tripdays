How Many Days in Paris?

5-7 days recommended

Why Visit Paris?

Paris, known as the City of Light, is a dream destination that combines rich history, world-class art, exquisite cuisine, and romantic ambiance. From the iconic Eiffel Tower to the magnificent Louvre Museum, Paris offers an unparalleled blend of architectural marvels, cultural treasures, and charming neighborhoods. The city's elegant boulevards, scenic Seine River, and sidewalk cafés create a magical atmosphere that captivates visitors. Whether you're admiring masterpieces in renowned museums, savoring delicious pastries at local bakeries, or simply strolling through picturesque gardens, Paris promises an unforgettable experience that justifies its reputation as one of the world's most beautiful cities.

How Many Days Do You Need in Paris?

To truly experience Paris, we recommend 5-7 days. This duration allows you to visit the major attractions without rushing, explore different neighborhoods, enjoy the cuisine, and even take a day trip to nearby Versailles. If you only have 3-4 days, you can still see the highlights, but you'll need to prioritize. With a week or more, you can immerse yourself in the Parisian lifestyle and discover hidden gems beyond the tourist circuit.

Paris Itinerary: Day by Day

Day 1: Iconic Paris

Start your Paris adventure with its most famous landmarks. Begin at the Eiffel Tower (book tickets in advance to avoid lines), then stroll through the Champ de Mars gardens. Cross the Seine via Pont d'Iéna to reach Trocadéro for spectacular tower views. Continue to the Arc de Triomphe and walk down the elegant Champs-Élysées. End your day at Place de la Concorde and the beautiful Tuileries Garden.

Day 2: Museums and Art

Dedicate this day to world-class art. Arrive early at the Louvre Museum to see the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and countless other masterpieces (plan at least 3-4 hours). After lunch, cross the Seine to visit the Musée d'Orsay, housed in a former railway station and home to an impressive collection of Impressionist paintings. If time permits, explore the Orangerie Museum to see Monet's Water Lilies.

Day 3: Historic Heart

Explore the historic islands at Paris's center. Start at Notre-Dame Cathedral on Île de la Cité (view from outside due to reconstruction). Visit the exquisite Sainte-Chapelle with its stunning stained glass, then the medieval Conciergerie. Cross to charming Île Saint-Louis for lunch and ice cream at Berthillon. Spend the afternoon in the Latin Quarter, visiting the Panthéon and Luxembourg Gardens.

Day 4: Montmartre and Bohemian Paris

Discover the artistic soul of Paris in Montmartre. Begin at the magnificent white Sacré-Cœur Basilica with panoramic city views. Wander through Montmartre's winding streets, past the Place du Tertre with its artists, and see the famous Moulin Rouge cabaret. In the afternoon, explore the trendy Canal Saint-Martin neighborhood with its boutiques, cafés, and picturesque waterway.

Day 5: Royal Paris

Experience Paris's royal heritage at the Palace of Versailles (a day trip). Explore the opulent palace, the Hall of Mirrors, and the extensive gardens. Return to Paris in the late afternoon and spend the evening in the Marais district, known for its preserved medieval architecture, trendy shops, and excellent dining options.

Day 6: Modern Paris

Discover contemporary Paris at the Centre Pompidou with its inside-out architecture and modern art collection. Then head to the revitalized Parc de la Villette area to visit the Cité des Sciences et de l'Industrie (Science Museum). In the evening, explore the vibrant Bastille area with its many restaurants and bars.

Day 7: Hidden Gems

On your final day, discover lesser-known treasures. Visit the atmospheric Père Lachaise Cemetery, final resting place of many famous figures. Explore the Buttes-Chaumont Park with its dramatic landscapes and temple. Browse the vintage shops and cafés in the Belleville neighborhood. End your Paris adventure with a sunset Seine River cruise to see the city's illuminated monuments.

Top Attractions in Paris

  • Eiffel Tower - The iconic symbol of Paris offering panoramic views
  • Louvre Museum - World's largest art museum and home to the Mona Lisa
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral - Gothic masterpiece (currently under restoration)
  • Sacré-Cœur Basilica - White-domed church with stunning city views
  • Champs-Élysées & Arc de Triomphe - Famous avenue leading to the triumphal arch
  • Musée d'Orsay - Impressive collection of Impressionist art
  • Palace of Versailles - Opulent royal château just outside Paris
  • Seine River - For scenic walks and boat cruises
  • Montmartre - Bohemian hilltop neighborhood with artistic heritage
  • Le Marais - Historic district with preserved medieval buildings

Practical Tips for Visiting Paris

Best Time to Visit

The ideal times to visit Paris are April to June or September to October when the weather is pleasant and crowds are smaller. Summer (July-August) is peak tourist season with longer lines and higher prices, while many locals are away on vacation. Winter offers fewer crowds and festive decorations, but shorter days and cooler temperatures.

Getting Around

Paris has an excellent public transportation system. The Metro is the fastest way to get around, with stations near most attractions. Consider purchasing a Paris Visite pass for unlimited travel. Walking is ideal for exploring neighborhoods, while Vélib' bike rentals offer another pleasant option in good weather. Taxis and rideshares are available but traffic can be heavy.

Budget Planning

Expect to spend about €100-150 per day per person for mid-range travel, including accommodation, meals, attractions, and transportation. Save money by visiting museums on the first Sunday of the month (free admission), purchasing the Paris Museum Pass for multiple attractions, and enjoying picnics in parks with food from local markets. Budget accommodations start around €80 per night, while mid-range hotels range from €120-200.

Local Etiquette

Always greet shop owners and restaurant staff with "Bonjour" (hello) or "Bonsoir" (good evening) when entering. Learn a few basic French phrases - locals appreciate the effort. Tipping is not required as service is included in restaurant bills, but rounding up or leaving a few euros for good service is appreciated.

Is Paris Worth Visiting?

Paris lives up to its reputation as one of the world's most beautiful and culturally rich cities. With its blend of iconic landmarks, world-class museums, charming neighborhoods, and exceptional cuisine, Paris offers something for every traveler. While 5-7 days allows for a comprehensive experience, even a shorter visit will leave you with unforgettable memories and likely a desire to return.
