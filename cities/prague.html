How many days to spend in Prague

Prague, with its medieval charm, cobblestone streets, and stunning architecture, feels like stepping into a storybook. To fully experience Prague, we recommend spending 3–5 days.

With 3–5 days, you can explore Prague Castle, wander across Charles Bridge, visit the Old Town Square, and take a day trip to Kutná Hora or Český Krumlov. This duration lets you soak in Prague’s magical atmosphere.

Suggested Itinerary for Prague

Here’s a suggested itinerary for 3–5 days in Prague. Days 1–3 cover the essentials, while Days 4–5 are perfect for an extended stay.

Day 1: Arrival and Old Town

Arrive in Prague and explore the Old Town Square—see the Astronomical Clock and Týn Church. Stroll across Charles Bridge at sunset and enjoy dinner at a local restaurant.

Day 2: Prague Castle and Malá Strana

Visit Prague Castle, including St. Vitus Cathedral. Wander through the charming Malá Strana neighborhood and relax at a café. In the evening, explore the Vyšehrad fortress.

Day 3: Jewish Quarter and Vltava River

Explore the Jewish Quarter—visit the Old Jewish Cemetery and Spanish Synagogue. In the afternoon, take a boat cruise on the Vltava River to see Prague from the water.

Day 4: Day Trip to Kutná Hora Extended Stay

Take a day trip to Kutná Hora to visit the Bone Church (Sedlec Ossuary) and St. Barbara’s Cathedral. Return to Prague for a relaxed evening.

Day 5: Leisure and Departure Extended Stay

Spend your final day visiting the Letná Park for views of the city or shopping for souvenirs in the Old Town. Depart from Prague with unforgettable memories.

Local Tips for Visiting Prague

  • Buy a Prague Card for free entry to major attractions like Prague Castle and public transport access.
  • Try Czech classics like goulash, svíčková (marinated beef), or trdelník (a sweet pastry) from street vendors.
  • Visit Charles Bridge early in the morning to avoid crowds and enjoy a peaceful experience.
  • Be cautious of currency exchange scams—use ATMs or reputable exchange offices instead of street vendors.
  • Bring comfortable shoes—Prague’s cobblestone streets can be tough on your feet.

Practical Information for Prague

Getting Around

Prague’s trams, metro, and buses are efficient—buy a day pass or Prague Card for unlimited travel. The city is also very walkable, especially in the historic center.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to May) and autumn (September to October) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer is busy, while winter (especially December) is magical with Christmas markets.

Safety Tips

Prague is very safe, but watch for pickpockets in crowded areas like Old Town Square and Charles Bridge. Avoid unofficial taxis and use apps like Uber or Bolt instead.
