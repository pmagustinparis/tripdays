How many days to spend in Cape Town

Cape Town, with its dramatic landscapes, vibrant culture, and rich history, is a gem of South Africa. To fully experience Cape Town, we recommend spending 5–7 days.

With 5–7 days, you can hike Table Mountain, explore the Cape Peninsula, visit Robben Island, and take a day trip to the Winelands. This duration lets you enjoy both the city’s natural beauty and cultural attractions.

Suggested Itinerary for Cape Town

Here’s a suggested itinerary for 5–7 days in Cape Town. Days 1–5 cover the essentials, while Days 6–7 are perfect for an extended stay.

Day 1: Arrival and Table Mountain

Arrive in Cape Town and take the cable car up Table Mountain for panoramic views. Explore the city center and enjoy dinner at the V&A Waterfront.

Day 2: Cape Peninsula Tour

Drive to the Cape of Good Hope and Cape Point. Stop at Boulders Beach to see the African penguins and enjoy the scenic Chapman’s Peak Drive.

Day 3: Robben Island and City Exploration

Take a ferry to Robben Island to learn about its history. In the afternoon, explore the colorful Bo-Kaap neighborhood and visit the District Six Museum.

Day 4: Day Trip to the Winelands

Visit Stellenbosch and Franschhoek for wine tasting and stunning vineyard views. Enjoy a leisurely lunch at a wine estate before returning to Cape Town.

Day 5: Kirstenbosch and Beaches

Spend the morning at Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens. In the afternoon, relax at Camps Bay or Clifton Beach and watch the sunset.

Day 6: Hidden Gems Extended Stay

Explore the Woodstock neighborhood for street art and trendy cafés. Then hike Lion’s Head for a less crowded alternative to Table Mountain with incredible views.

Day 7: Leisure and Departure Extended Stay

Spend your final day shopping at Greenmarket Square or visiting the Zeitz MOCAA museum. Depart from Cape Town with unforgettable memories.

Local Tips for Visiting Cape Town

  • Check the weather before hiking Table Mountain—strong winds can close the cable car, so have a backup plan.
  • Rent a car for flexibility, especially for day trips to the Cape Peninsula and Winelands.
  • Try local dishes like bobotie (a spiced meat dish) or a Gatsby (a loaded sandwich) at a local eatery.
  • Be cautious with valuables at popular tourist spots like the V&A Waterfront and beaches.
  • Book Robben Island tickets in advance, as they often sell out, especially during peak season.

Practical Information for Cape Town

Getting Around

Renting a car is the best way to explore Cape Town and its surroundings. Public transport like MyCiTi buses is reliable in the city, and rideshare apps like Uber are widely available.

Best Time to Visit

Summer (December to February) offers warm, dry weather, perfect for beaches and outdoor activities. Spring (September to November) and autumn (March to May) are quieter with mild temperatures.

Safety Tips

Cape Town is generally safe for tourists, but avoid walking alone at night in isolated areas. Stick to well-populated areas and be cautious of petty theft in busy spots.
