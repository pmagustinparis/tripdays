How many days to spend in London

London, a vibrant city blending history and modernity, offers endless attractions from royal palaces to bustling markets. To fully experience London, we recommend spending 5–7 days.

With 5–7 days, you can explore Buckingham Palace, visit the British Museum, enjoy a West End show, and take a day trip to Stonehenge or Oxford. This timeframe allows you to soak in the city’s diverse culture and history.

Suggested Itinerary for London

Here’s a suggested itinerary for 5–7 days in London. Days 1–5 cover the essentials, while Days 6–7 are perfect for an extended stay.

Day 1: Arrival and Central London

Arrive in London, check into your accommodation, and start with a walk through Trafalgar Square. Visit the National Gallery and enjoy dinner in Covent Garden.

Day 2: Royal London

Watch the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace, then explore Westminster Abbey and the Houses of Parliament. End your day with a ride on the London Eye.

Day 3: History and Culture

Spend the morning at the British Museum, then visit the Tower of London to see the Crown Jewels. Take an evening stroll along the South Bank.

Day 4: Markets and Parks

Explore Camden Market in the morning, then relax in Hyde Park. In the afternoon, visit Kensington Palace and the Victoria and Albert Museum.

Day 5: Day Trip to Stonehenge

Take a day trip to Stonehenge and Bath. Explore the ancient stone circle and the Roman Baths, returning to London for a West End show in the evening.

Day 6: Hidden Gems Extended Stay

Discover Notting Hill and Portobello Road Market. Visit the Sherlock Holmes Museum and enjoy a traditional afternoon tea at a local café.

Day 7: Leisure and Departure Extended Stay

Spend your final day shopping on Oxford Street or visiting the Tate Modern. Depart from London with lifelong memories.

Local Tips for Visiting London

  • Get an Oyster card for easy travel on the Tube and buses—it’s cheaper than single tickets.
  • Londoners queue politely—whether at a bus stop or a café, join the line and wait your turn.
  • Visit museums like the British Museum and National Gallery—they’re free and perfect for a rainy day.
  • Book West End show tickets in advance for better seats and prices, or try the TKTS booth in Leicester Square for last-minute deals.
  • Bring an umbrella—London’s weather can be unpredictable, even in summer.

Practical Information for London

Getting Around

The London Underground (Tube) is the fastest way to get around. Use an Oyster card or contactless payment for convenience. Buses are also a great way to see the city.

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) offer mild weather and fewer crowds. Summer (June to August) is busy but great for festivals, while winter (December to February) brings festive lights and markets.

Safety Tips

London is generally safe, but watch for pickpockets in busy areas like Oxford Street and the Tube. Stick to well-lit areas at night, especially in less touristy neighborhoods.
