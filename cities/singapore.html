How many days to spend in Singapore

Singapore, a gleaming city-state, blends futuristic architecture, lush gardens, and diverse cultures. To fully experience Singapore, we recommend spending 3–5 days.

With 3–5 days, you can explore Marina Bay Sands, wander through Gardens by the Bay, visit Sentosa Island, and dive into the city’s hawker food scene. This duration lets you experience Singapore’s unique mix of modernity and tradition.

Suggested Itinerary for Singapore

Here’s a suggested itinerary for 3–5 days in Singapore. Days 1–3 cover the essentials, while Days 4–5 are perfect for an extended stay.

Day 1: Arrival and Marina Bay

Arrive in Singapore and start at Marina Bay Sands—visit the SkyPark Observation Deck. Explore Gardens by the Bay and watch the Supertree Grove light show in the evening.

Day 2: Sentosa Island

Spend the day on Sentosa Island—visit Universal Studios Singapore, relax at Palawan Beach, and explore S.E.A. Aquarium. Return to the city for dinner at a hawker center.

Day 3: Cultural Highlights

Explore Chinatown and visit the Buddha Tooth Relic Temple. Then head to Little India to see Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple. End the day with a stroll along Clarke Quay.

Day 4: Nature and Wildlife Extended Stay

Visit the Singapore Botanic Gardens, a UNESCO site, and explore the National Orchid Garden. In the afternoon, head to the Singapore Zoo or Night Safari.

Day 5: Leisure and Departure Extended Stay

Spend your final day shopping on Orchard Road or visiting Jewel Changi Airport to see the Rain Vortex. Depart from Singapore with unforgettable memories.

Local Tips for Visiting Singapore

  • Use an EZ-Link card for seamless travel on the MRT, buses, and even some taxis.
  • Eat at hawker centers like Lau Pa Sat or Newton Food Centre for affordable, delicious meals—try chicken rice or laksa.
  • Singapore is strict about cleanliness—don’t litter, and avoid chewing gum, as it’s banned.
  • Bring an umbrella or raincoat—Singapore’s tropical climate means sudden showers are common.
  • Book tickets for attractions like Marina Bay Sands SkyPark or Universal Studios in advance to avoid long queues.

Practical Information for Singapore

Getting Around

Singapore’s MRT is efficient and covers most attractions—use an EZ-Link card for convenience. Taxis and rideshare apps like Grab are also widely available.

Best Time to Visit

February to April offers pleasant weather with less rain. May to July can be hot, while November to January is the wettest season due to the monsoon.

Safety Tips

Singapore is one of the safest cities in the world, but follow local laws—fines are steep for offenses like jaywalking or smoking in prohibited areas.
